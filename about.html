Our Mission

Psychologists for Perinatal Mental Health and Reproductive Justice brings together psychologists and mental health professionals committed to improving mental health care during pregnancy, birth, and postpartum — grounded in the principles of reproductive justice.

We believe that mental health care during the perinatal period is inseparable from the broader fight for bodily autonomy, equitable access to reproductive health care, and the dismantling of systemic barriers that disproportionately harm marginalized communities.

Our Values

Reproductive Justice

We root our work in the reproductive justice framework: the right to have children, to not have children, and to parent in safe and healthy environments.

Equity

We center the needs of Black, Indigenous, and communities of color, LGBTQ+ parents, and other groups historically underserved by perinatal mental health systems.

Evidence-Informed Care

We promote clinical practice and policy grounded in research, lived experience, and community accountability.

Collective Action

We believe lasting change happens through collaboration among clinicians, researchers, advocates, and the communities we serve.

Who We Are

Our members include clinical psychologists, researchers, trainees, and allied mental health professionals working across clinical, academic, and community settings. We welcome anyone who shares our commitment to perinatal mental health and reproductive justice.
